WEATHERGUARD VS. KARGOMASTER.

Capital Upfitters Team · April 2026 7 min read

If you're upfitting a cargo van for a trade business in the DMV area, two brands dominate the conversation: Weatherguard and Kargomaster. Both are authorized brands we install. Both are quality systems. But they're built differently, and the right one depends on how you work.

Weatherguard: The Industry Standard for Heavy Use

Weatherguard has been the go-to shelving system for utility companies, municipalities, and heavy-trade contractors for decades. Their systems are built in steel and aluminum combinations — heavy-gauge, welded, built to last 20+ years in daily use.

Who uses Weatherguard:

  • Utility and infrastructure crews (Pepco, Washington Gas, WSSC contractors)
  • Government and municipal fleet operators
  • Heavy-equipment maintenance and repair shops
  • Contractors who carry extremely heavy tools (pipe, conduit, heavy power tools)

Typical Weatherguard build:

  • Steel modular shelf units with welded frames
  • Locking drawer systems for small parts and valuable tools
  • Bin rails and slotted dividers for organized small parts
  • Steel bulkhead / cab partition separating cargo area from cab

The tradeoff: a fully loaded Weatherguard steel system adds approximately 150–200 lbs to the van. For a Ford Transit or Sprinter already heavy with tools and materials, this weight is meaningful — it reduces payload capacity and affects fuel economy on longer routes.

Basic Weatherguard van shelving package starts at $1,500 installed. Full upfit with bulkhead, ladder rack, and floor liner from $3,500. Call us for a fleet consultation.

Kargomaster: Modular Aluminum for the Modern Trade Fleet

Kargomaster is an all-aluminum modular shelving system engineered specifically for cargo van bodies. The aluminum construction makes it significantly lighter than steel equivalents — a comparable Kargomaster setup typically adds 80–120 lbs versus 150–200 lbs for Weatherguard steel.

Who chooses Kargomaster:

  • HVAC, electrical, and plumbing contractors (trade-standard workflow, frequent reconfiguration)
  • Multi-van fleets where fuel cost is tracked per vehicle
  • Service businesses that reconfigure shelving seasonally or as the business grows
  • Sprinter and Transit operators with high base vehicle cost who want to protect payload

The key advantage of Kargomaster's modular design is reconfigurability. As your business changes — new services, different tool inventory, adding staff — the shelving adapts without requiring a full reinstall. Individual modules can be added, removed, or repositioned without specialized tools.

Side-by-Side Comparison

  • Material: Weatherguard (steel/aluminum combo) vs. Kargomaster (all-aluminum)
  • Weight added: Weatherguard 150–200 lbs vs. Kargomaster 80–120 lbs
  • Reconfigurability: Weatherguard (modular but heavier to move) vs. Kargomaster (fully modular, easy)
  • Best for heavy tools: Weatherguard (steel frame holds more per shelf)
  • Best for weight-conscious: Kargomaster
  • Typical warranty: Both carry manufacturer warranties; Weatherguard has longer track record

Our Recommendation by Trade

  • HVAC and plumbing → Kargomaster. You reconfigure frequently, carry medium-weight parts, and run multiple vans.
  • Electrical → Either works well. Kargomaster for lighter wire spools; Weatherguard for heavy conduit and benders.
  • General contractor / heavy equipment → Weatherguard steel. The weight penalty is worth the durability.
  • Government / municipal → Weatherguard. It's what fleet managers specify in procurement contracts.
  • Landscaping / pest control → Kargomaster. Light weight, easy cleaning, reconfigure for seasonal kit changes.
