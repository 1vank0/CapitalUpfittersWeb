Know Before You Tint

MARYLAND TINT LAW.

Maryland has specific VLT requirements for each window position. We check every install before you leave.

Maryland Window Tint Law — What You Need to Know
Windshield
Non-reflective tint on top 5 inches only (AS-1 line). Full windshield tint is illegal.
Front Side Windows
Must allow MORE than 35% VLT (light in). This is the most common mistake.
Rear Side Windows
Any darkness allowed — 35% or darker is legal with no restriction.
Rear Window
Any darkness allowed if the vehicle has dual side mirrors. No VLT minimum.

Reflectivity rule: No more than 35% reflective on any window. We do not install high-reflective metallic films.  |  VLT explained: VLT = Visible Light Transmission. 35% VLT means the window lets in 35% of visible light — darker film, lower number. 70% VLT is nearly clear.

All Capital Upfitters tint installations comply with Maryland law. We will not install illegal tint — it protects you from failed inspections and traffic stops.
Why Tint Your Windows

WHY TINT YOUR WINDOWS?

The right film does far more than darken glass. Here's what quality window tinting delivers for Maryland drivers.

Heat Rejection

Quality tint film blocks up to 60% of solar heat in Maryland summers. Your AC works less, your interior stays cooler, and leather seats don't burn you.

UV Protection

UV rays damage dashboards, fade upholstery, and accelerate leather cracking. Ceramic film blocks 99% of UV — protecting both your interior and passengers.

Privacy & Security

Tinted windows deter smash-and-grab theft by obscuring interior contents. Privacy matters whether you're parked at a Rockville parking garage or a Bethesda trail head.

FAQ

COMMON QUESTIONS.

Maryland allows tinting on all windows EXCEPT the windshield (only the top 5 inches). Front side windows must transmit more than 35% visible light (VLT). Rear side windows and rear window can be any darkness. All our installs comply with Maryland law — we won't install illegal percentages.

Standard dyed or carbon film installs typically take 1.5–3 hours depending on vehicle size. Ceramic film takes slightly longer due to precision handling. Most vehicles are ready same-day.

Dyed film provides basic privacy and glare reduction. Carbon film adds better heat rejection and a matte appearance that doesn't fade. Ceramic film uses nano-ceramic particles for maximum heat (60%) and UV (99%) rejection without any color or signal interference. Ceramic is significantly more effective in Maryland summers.

Carbon and ceramic films do not interfere with any electronics. Older metallic films can cause GPS and cell signal issues — we do not install metallic films for this reason.

Window tint fully cures in 3–7 days. You may see slight haziness or small water bubbles during this period — this is normal and will disappear as the film cures. Avoid rolling windows down for the first 3 days.
